OVERVIEW

OBJECTIVE

​Information graphics are widely used to convey messages and present insights in data effectively. However, creating expressive data-driven infographics remains a great challenge for general users without design expertise. Our target is to enables users to easily create data-driven infographics using our visualization design tool. Not only for graphic designers or visualization experts, but also for data reports or business dashboards. After releasing version 1.0 on Microsoft Office Store, we want to evaluate its usability and utility for general users.

LITERATURE REVIEW

Information Graphics or plain charts?

Traditionally, visual embellishment are considered harmful. Visualizations are kept plain for the effectiveness of data analysis. However, recently, researchers find infographics can enhance data communication:
(1) Embellished charts have higher recall and can increase memorability.
(2) Figurative elements can effectively provide context of data.


Method for enhancing data communication

Visual embellishment: Pictographic elements, textual annotations, elaborately designed figures and imageries.

COMPETITIVE ANALYSIS

We did competitive analysis for different categories’ visualization tools. Based on the advantages and disadvantages of different categories, we summarized the characteristic we would like to have for our tools:
- Use mark customization approach: Just start from a traditional chart, and use click to finish everything.
- Highly functional customizations to provide flexible.
- Integrate into existing analysis and exploration system to smooth the iteration of data exploration, analysis and presentation.

User Survey

Why This Method?

To get a more comprehensive understanding about how InfoNice is used in real-world scenarios, we further conducted an online survey on InfoNice end-users.


Survey Design

(Click here to see more details about Survey Design)

The survey is consists of three sections:
- Overall functionality and usability experience: Effectiveness (in conveying messages), powerfulness (of creating infographics), aesthetics, ease of use, ease of learning, and satisfaction.
- Features: Rate individual features regarding how helpful they are.
- Usage: Provide information about their jobs and usage.


Real World Feedback

With the public release of InfoNice to Microsoft Power BI users, we provided a customer-support email address and encouraged users to send their feedback. In total, we have received 118 emails from 94 users by the end of July 2017. The users explicitly express positive comments in 108 emails.
